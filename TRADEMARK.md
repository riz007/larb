# Larb Trademark Policy

The Apache-2.0 license covers Larb's **source code**. It does **not** grant any
right to use the **"Larb" name or logo** as a trademark. This policy explains
permitted and restricted uses, so the community can fork freely while users can
still trust what "Larb" refers to.

> Note: "larb" (also "laab"/"larp") is a well-known Lao/Thai dish, so the bare
> word may be hard to register as a trademark in some jurisdictions. This policy
> asserts the project's mark in the software field; consult counsel before
> relying on it commercially.

## You may, without asking

- State that your project **works with**, is **built on**, is **compatible
  with**, or is a **fork of** Larb — factually and without implying endorsement.
- Use the name in articles, talks, tutorials, and reviews.
- Keep the name in unmodified copies you redistribute (attribution is required
  by the license and NOTICE).

## Please do not, without written permission

- Use "Larb" (or a confusingly similar name) as the **name of your product,
  service, fork, or company**, or in a way that implies it is the official
  project or that the Larb Authors endorse it.
- Use the Larb name or logo on **paid or hosted offerings** in a way that could
  confuse users about the source.
- Modify the logo, or use it as your own application/marketplace icon.

## Naming a fork or derivative

Give it a clearly distinct name. "Built on Larb" or "a Larb fork" is fine as a
**description**; it must not be the product **name**.

## Questions / requests

Open an issue at https://github.com/riz007/larb/issues or contact the
maintainers. We grant reasonable requests, especially for community and
educational use.
